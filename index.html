Discover Ireland's Ancient East


Trim, located in County Meath, Ireland, boasts a rich history and is home to the magnificent Trim Castle, a medieval fortress that stands as an emblem of Norman strength. The town's skyline is adorned by The Yellow Steeple, a striking bell tower dating back to the 14th century. Additionally, the ruins of The Priory of St. John the Baptist add to Trim's historical allure, providing a glimpse into its medieval past and contributing to the town's status as a captivating destination for history enthusiasts.

Trim, Co. Meath

In the year 1000 AD, Trim in County Meath, Ireland, thrived as a bustling medieval settlement with a strategic significance. Trim Castle, constructed by the Normans in the late 12th century, was yet to dominate the skyline. The town, nestled along the Boyne River, likely featured a network of wooden structures, modest dwellings, and artisan workshops. Trim's early medieval landscape, dotted with religious sites like the Priory of St. John the Baptist, underscored the town's cultural and religious importance in this era, setting the stage for its enduring historical legacy.

Trim Castle

Trim Castle traces its origins to the late 12th century when it was commissioned by Hugh de Lacy, a Norman lord, in 1176. As a strategic stronghold, the castle played a pivotal role in securing Norman control over the region. With its imposing walls and majestic keep, Trim Castle stands as a testament to medieval engineering and remains one of the finest examples of Anglo-Norman military architecture in Ireland.

The Priory of St. John the Baptist

The Priory of St. John the Baptist in Trim, County Meath, finds its origins in the early medieval period, with its foundation attributed to the Knights Hospitaller in the 13th century. Established to serve as a religious and hospitaller institution, the priory played a vital role in the community, providing spiritual guidance and charitable services. The ruins of this historic site offer glimpses into the religious practices and societal functions of medieval Trim.

The Yellow Steeple

The Yellow Steeple, a distinctive landmark in Trim, County Meath, Ireland, has its origins dating back to the late 14th century. Originally part of St. Mary's Abbey, the tower served as a bell tower for the adjacent church. Destroyed in the 16th century during the Dissolution of the Monasteries, the Yellow Steeple remains a remarkable remnant of medieval architecture, with its distinct ochre hue contributing to its modern-day charm and historical significance.
